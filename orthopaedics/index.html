Common Symptoms We Treat

Musculoskeletal pain is one of the most common reasons patients visit a healthcare provider. Recognising symptoms early and consulting a bone specialist can prevent minor issues from becoming chronic conditions. Our orthopaedic doctors in Ullal evaluate and treat the following symptoms:

Persistent Joint Pain

Chronic aching in the knees, hips, shoulders, or wrists that lasts more than a few days, worsens with activity, or disrupts daily routines and sleep.

Swelling & Stiffness

Visible swelling around joints, morning stiffness lasting over 30 minutes, or difficulty fully bending or straightening a limb — often signs of arthritis or injury.

Difficulty Walking or Standing

Limping, instability, or pain while bearing weight can indicate fractures, ligament tears, hip joint degeneration, or spinal nerve compression.

Numbness & Tingling

Pins-and-needles sensation, numbness in hands or feet, or radiating pain down the arms or legs may signal a pinched nerve, slipped disc, or carpal tunnel syndrome.

Muscle Weakness

Progressive weakness in the arms, legs, or grip strength that impairs daily activities. This can result from nerve damage, rotator cuff tears, or spinal disorders.

Back & Neck Pain

Persistent lower back pain, cervical stiffness, or radiating sciatica that worsens with sitting, bending, or coughing — common indicators of disc or vertebral problems.

Don't ignore persistent bone or joint symptoms. Early diagnosis leads to better outcomes — consult our orthopaedic team now.

Our Diagnostic Approach

Accurate diagnosis is the foundation of effective orthopaedic treatment. At Hermes Health Care, our bone specialists follow a structured, evidence-based diagnostic protocol to identify the root cause of musculoskeletal complaints before recommending any treatment. Here is how our diagnostic process works:

  1. Detailed Clinical History: Our orthopaedic doctor begins with a thorough patient interview covering symptom onset, duration, aggravating factors, past injuries, family history of joint diseases, and current medications.
  2. Physical Examination: A hands-on assessment of joint range of motion, muscle strength, reflexes, gait analysis, and specific orthopaedic tests such as the drawer test for knee ligaments or the straight leg raise for disc problems.
  3. Diagnostic Imaging: X-rays to assess bone alignment and fractures, and referral for MRI or CT scans when soft tissue evaluation, disc herniation, or ligament tears require detailed visualisation.
  4. Laboratory Tests: Blood tests for inflammatory markers (ESR, CRP), uric acid for gout, rheumatoid factor, calcium levels, and vitamin D to identify underlying metabolic or autoimmune causes via our in-house diagnostic laboratory.
  5. Personalised Treatment Plan: Based on combined findings, our orthopaedic team creates a tailored treatment strategy — conservative, interventional, or surgical — with clear recovery milestones and follow-up schedules.

This systematic approach ensures that patients receive the most appropriate care. Whether it is a simple muscle strain or a complex spinal condition, every patient at our Ullal clinic receives the same thorough evaluation process. For patients who also need general medical assessments, our multi-speciality setup allows seamless coordination between departments.

Treatment Options at Hermes Health Care

Our orthopaedic department offers a full range of treatment modalities, from conservative management to surgical interventions. The choice of treatment depends on the specific condition, its severity, the patient's overall health, and their recovery goals. Our surgeons always discuss treatment options thoroughly with patients and their families.

Treatment Methods Available at Our Orthopaedic Clinic

  • Medications: Pain relievers, anti-inflammatory drugs, muscle relaxants, and disease-modifying agents for arthritis
  • Joint Injections: Corticosteroid injections and viscosupplementation (hyaluronic acid) for knee and shoulder joints
  • Fracture Fixation: Plaster casts, functional braces, and surgical internal fixation with plates, screws, or intramedullary nails
  • Arthroscopy: Minimally invasive camera-guided surgery for knee, shoulder, and ankle joint repairs
  • Joint Replacement Consultations: Pre-surgical evaluations for total and partial knee and hip replacements
  • Ligament Reconstruction: ACL repair, meniscal repair, and rotator cuff reconstruction procedures
  • Spine Care: Conservative disc management, traction therapy, nerve root blocks, and surgical referrals when indicated
  • Physiotherapy Coordination: Post-treatment rehabilitation through our in-house physiotherapy centre

Frequently Asked Questions

What does an orthopaedic doctor treat?

An orthopaedic doctor treats musculoskeletal disorders including bones, joints, ligaments, tendons, and nerves. Common conditions include fractures, arthritis, sports injuries, back pain, and spinal disorders. At Hermes Health Care in Ullal, our specialists manage both surgical and conservative orthopaedic treatments.

When should I see an orthopaedic specialist?

Consult an orthopaedic specialist if you have persistent joint pain lasting more than a few days, swelling around joints, limited range of motion, difficulty walking, numbness in limbs, or pain following an injury. Early consultation at our Ullal clinic helps prevent complications and chronic damage.

What are the common causes of knee pain?

Common causes include osteoarthritis, ligament tears like ACL or meniscus injuries, tendinitis, bursitis, runner's knee, and cartilage wear. Obesity and sports overuse also contribute significantly. Our bone specialists in Ullal use X-rays and MRI referrals to accurately diagnose the underlying cause.

How is a fracture treated without surgery?

Many fractures heal with plaster casts, fibreglass splints, or functional braces that immobilise the bone during healing. Pain management and monitored rehabilitation exercises support recovery. Our orthopaedic team evaluates fracture severity through imaging before recommending conservative or surgical treatment at our Bengaluru clinic.

What is the recovery time after knee replacement?

Basic mobility typically returns within six to twelve weeks, with full recovery taking three to six months. Supervised physiotherapy, gradual weight-bearing exercises, and regular follow-ups are essential. Hermes Health Care coordinates post-operative rehabilitation with our in-house physiotherapy team for optimal recovery outcomes.

What is the difference between orthopaedic and physiotherapy?

Orthopaedic care involves medical diagnosis, prescriptions, injections, and surgical interventions for bone and joint problems. Physiotherapy uses exercises, manual therapy, and mobility training for rehabilitation. What are the symptoms of a slipped disc?

Symptoms include sharp lower back pain, radiating leg pain called sciatica, numbness or tingling in the feet, muscle weakness, and difficulty bending the back. Pain often worsens when coughing or sitting for long periods. Our spine specialists at Hermes Health Care diagnose disc problems using MRI imaging.

Can orthopaedic problems be treated without surgery?

Yes, many orthopaedic conditions respond effectively to conservative treatments including medications, physiotherapy, joint injections, bracing, and lifestyle changes. At Hermes Health Care, our doctors explore non-surgical options first and recommend surgery only when it is clinically necessary for the best long-term outcome.

What is arthroscopy surgery?

Arthroscopy is a minimally invasive procedure where a small camera is inserted into the joint through tiny incisions. It allows surgeons to diagnose and repair problems with less tissue damage, reduced pain, and faster recovery compared to open surgery. Dr. Santosh H R performs arthroscopic procedures at our clinic.

What exercises help with back pain?

Gentle stretching, pelvic tilts, cat-cow stretches, partial crunches, walking, swimming, and yoga can help relieve back pain. These strengthen core muscles supporting the spine.
