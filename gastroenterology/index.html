A gastroenterologist diagnoses and treats disorders of the digestive tract, which includes the esophagus, stomach, small intestine, colon, gallbladder, pancreas, and liver.

Consult a specialist for symptoms like chronic heartburn or acidity, persistent abdominal pain, ongoing bloating, frequent diarrhea or constipation, blood in stools, or unexplained yellowing of skin (jaundice).

Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS) causes abdominal pain, cramping, bloating, excess gas, and alterations in bowel habits (alternating episodes of diarrhea and constipation).

Acidity refers to acid reflux causing transient heartburn. Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease (GERD) is a chronic, severe form of reflux occurring more than twice a week, potentially causing esophageal tissue damage.

Liver problems are commonly caused by fatty liver disease (NAFLD), excessive alcohol consumption, viral hepatitis infections, autoimmune conditions, or the toxic buildup of heavy medications.
